Profile

Research focus

Current work.

My doctoral research attacks and defends the intrusion detection systems that protect in-vehicle networks.

01

Adversarial attacks on CAN-bus IDS

A robustness framework that generates inputs which slip past in-vehicle intrusion detection while preserving physical plausibility — perturbations that would survive on a real bus, not only in simulation.

02

Validation in CARLA

The approach is implemented and tested in the CARLA autonomous-driving simulator and evaluated across multiple IDS architectures to characterise where each is vulnerable.

03

Defenses for edge AI

Ongoing work translates these vulnerability findings into defense frameworks, with emphasis on securing edge-based AI deployments under tight compute and latency budgets.

04

PortSec — authentication in SDN

A communication protocol using port knocking as an authentication mechanism in SDN-enabled networks, implemented and tested in P4 on a BMv2 virtual switch.
